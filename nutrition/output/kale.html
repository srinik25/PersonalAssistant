📜 History & Origin

Kale is a leafy green vegetable that has been cultivated for over 2,000 years. It was a staple in the diets of ancient Greeks and Romans and has been a popular vegetable in Europe since the Middle Ages.

⏰ Best Time to Eat

Kale can be consumed at any time of the day, but incorporating it into lunch or dinner can enhance nutrient absorption due to its fat-soluble vitamins.

🍽 Meal Incorporation

Can be added to meals: Yes

  • Smoothies: Blend 1 cup of kale with fruits for a nutrient boost.
  • Salads: Use as a base for salads with a lemon-tahini dressing.
  • Soups: Add chopped kale to vegetable soups for added nutrition.

Additional Notes

  • Massaging kale with a little olive oil and lemon juice can help break down the fibers, making it more palatable and easier to digest.
  • Pairing kale with a source of healthy fat, like avocado or nuts, enhances the absorption of fat-soluble vitamins A, E, and K.
  • Store kale in the refrigerator in a plastic bag with a paper towel to absorb moisture and extend freshness.

🍳 Recipe: Vegan Kale and Quinoa Salad

A refreshing and nutrient-packed salad perfect for a light lunch or side dish.

⏲ Prep 15 minutes
🔥 Cook 15 minutes
🍴 Servings 4

Ingredients

  • 1 cup quinoa
  • 2 cups water
  • 1 bunch kale, stems removed and leaves chopped
  • 1/4 cup lemon juice
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1/2 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1/4 cup sunflower seeds
  • 1 avocado, diced

Instructions

  1. Rinse quinoa under cold water. In a medium saucepan, combine quinoa and water. Bring to a boil, reduce heat, cover, and simmer for 15 minutes or until water is absorbed. Fluff with a fork and let cool.
  2. In a large bowl, combine chopped kale, lemon juice, olive oil, salt, and pepper. Massage the kale with your hands until it softens.
  3. Add the cooled quinoa, cherry tomatoes, sunflower seeds, and avocado to the kale. Toss to combine.
  4. Serve immediately or refrigerate for up to 2 days.
