📜 History & Origin

Lentils are one of the oldest cultivated crops, with evidence of their consumption dating back to 9,000 to 13,000 years ago in the Near East. They have been a staple in Indian, Middle Eastern, and Mediterranean cuisines for centuries.

⏰ Best Time to Eat

Lentils can be consumed at any time of the day, but they are particularly beneficial for lunch or dinner to provide sustained energy and satiety.

🍽 Meal Incorporation

Can be added to meals: Yes

  • Soups: Add cooked lentils to vegetable soups for extra protein.
  • Salads: Toss cooked lentils with fresh vegetables and a vinaigrette.
  • Curries: Use lentils as a base for Indian-style curries.

Additional Notes

  • Soaking lentils for a few hours before cooking can reduce cooking time and improve digestibility.
  • Pair lentils with vitamin C-rich foods like tomatoes or bell peppers to enhance iron absorption.
  • Store dried lentils in an airtight container in a cool, dry place for up to a year.

🍳 Recipe: Vegan Lentil Curry

A hearty and flavorful vegan curry packed with protein and fiber.

⏲ Prep 10 minutes
🔥 Cook 30 minutes
🍴 Servings 4

Ingredients

  • 1 cup dried lentils
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 tablespoon ginger, grated
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 1 teaspoon coriander
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric
  • 1/2 teaspoon chili powder
  • 1 can (14 oz) diced tomatoes
  • 2 cups vegetable broth
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh cilantro for garnish

Instructions

  1. Rinse lentils under cold water and set aside.
  2. In a large pot, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add onion and sauté until translucent.
  3. Add garlic and ginger, cook for another minute.
  4. Stir in cumin, coriander, turmeric, and chili powder, cooking for 30 seconds until fragrant.
  5. Add diced tomatoes, vegetable broth, and lentils. Bring to a boil.
  6. Reduce heat to low, cover, and simmer for 25-30 minutes, or until lentils are tender.
  7. Season with salt to taste and garnish with fresh cilantro before serving.
